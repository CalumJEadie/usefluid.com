FAQ

Who are you?

We’re Andrew and Calum. We use sketches to communicate every day. Andrew understands both sides of the coin; he read English at St Andrews and has worked as a graphic designer. Calum studied Computer Science at Cambridge where he created a Visual Programming Language for the Raspberry Pi.

We share a deep empathy for how all kinds of people interact with technology and enthusiasm for attacking user experience problems with design thinking.

Why don’t more people sketch?

We don’t know yet. Some people don’t sketch because they feel they’re not allowed to in their job or it’s not what adults do. These aren’t very good answers. Sketching is something that anyone in an organisation, from sales to engineering, should be confident doing. Pen and paper is one of the most powerful tools for problem solving.

How workplaces can quash creativity is a widely reported phenomenon. We’d highly recommend David and Tom Kelley’s recently published book Creative Confidence if you’re interested in encouraging more creativity in your team.
